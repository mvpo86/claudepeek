About this tool

📊

A usage guide for your team, not a billing tool

This dashboard reads your local Claude Code conversation history and gives you a visual breakdown of how tokens and estimated costs are distributed across days, sessions, and models.

The goal is to help your team understand usage patterns, spot heavy usage days, and make informed decisions about which models to use — not to produce invoice figures.

⚠ Important: these are estimates, not your actual bill

Every cost figure shown in this tool is calculated by multiplying your token counts by Anthropic's public list prices. Your actual invoice from Anthropic may differ because:

  • Your local files store costUSD: null — no actual billed amount is ever written to disk by Claude Code
  • Anthropic may apply credits, volume discounts, or promotional rates to your account
  • List prices change over time; this tool applies today's prices to all historical usage
  • Cache pricing has multiple tiers (5-min vs 1-hour ephemeral) that may be approximated

For your real bill, always go to console.anthropic.com → Billing.

🔍

What the numbers mean

All ~$ figures are estimated costs derived from token usage. The tilde (~) prefix throughout the app is a reminder of this.

Token counts, on the other hand, are exact — they come directly from the API responses stored in your local ~/.claude/projects/ files.

📅

Sessions vs Daily

Sessions show the all-time accumulated cost for each project folder, regardless of any date filter you set. A session is one working directory.

Daily/Monthly views show what happened within a specific time window — use these to understand your recent spend rate.

🤖

Model tiers and cost

Opus is the most capable and most expensive. It's best for complex reasoning and architecture tasks.

Sonnet is the everyday workhorse — strong capability at moderate cost. Good default for most coding tasks.

Haiku is fast and cheap — great for routine edits, summaries, and high-volume operations.

💡

Tips for managing usage

Cache read tokens (shown in the Daily tab) are very cheap — heavy cache use is a sign of efficient conversations.

If a single day spikes, check which model was active. Switching from Opus to Sonnet can cut costs by ~80% for equivalent tasks.

Use the Budget tab to track your current period and get a projected end-of-month total.

🔧

How it works

A tiny local Node.js server reads your ~/.claude/projects/**/*.jsonl files via npx ccusage and exposes them as a JSON API.

No data leaves your machine. Nothing is sent to any external service. The UI is a single HTML file loaded from localhost:3737.

📦

Built on ccusage

The data is powered by ccusage, an open-source CLI tool that parses Claude Code's local conversation logs.

To start this dashboard: node server.js then open localhost:3737.
